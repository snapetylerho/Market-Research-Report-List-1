<p><p>Immersion cooling fluids are classified mainly into hydrocarbon and fluorocarbon types. Hydrocarbon fluids, derived from crude oil, are cost-effective and environmentally friendly, providing efficient thermal management for cooling applications, particularly in data centers. Fluorocarbon fluids, on the other hand, are synthetic compounds known for their thermal stability and non-flammability, making them suitable for high-performance computing environments. Both types offer distinct advantages, catering to varying industry needs and ensuring optimal heat dissipation in electronic systems.</p></p>
