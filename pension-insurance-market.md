<p><p>Pension insurance markets can be categorized into personal/private pensions and company/workplace pensions. Personal pensions are individual retirement plans that allow people to save independently, typically managed by financial institutions, providing flexibility in investment choices. In contrast, company or workplace pensions are established by employers to provide retirement benefits to their employees, often funded through salary deductions and employer contributions. Both types aim to ensure financial security in retirement, but differ in their structure and funding sources.</p></p>
