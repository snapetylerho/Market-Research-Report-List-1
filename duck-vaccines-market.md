<p><p>The duck vaccines market comprises two primary types: live vaccines and killed vaccines. Live vaccines contain attenuated or weakened pathogens that stimulate an immune response while maintaining virulence. They generally provide robust and long-lasting immunity. Conversely, killed vaccines consist of inactivated pathogens, ensuring safety with a reduced risk of disease induction. While killed vaccines require multiple doses for optimal effectiveness, they are often favored in environments where safety is paramount. Both types are essential for preventing diseases in domestic duck populations.</p></p>
