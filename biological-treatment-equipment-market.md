<p><p>Biological treatment equipment for waste management primarily includes dry fermentation and wet fermentation technologies. Dry fermentation involves the anaerobic digestion of solid organic waste, where microorganisms break down materials in a controlled environment, producing biogas and digestate. In contrast, wet fermentation processes utilize liquid substrates, allowing for easier mixing and enhanced microbial activity. Both techniques aim to convert organic waste into renewable energy and valuable by-products, but their application depends on the type and moisture content of the waste being processed.</p></p>
