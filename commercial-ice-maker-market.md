<p><p>The commercial ice maker market is primarily divided into two types: air-cooled and water-cooled ice makers. Air-cooled ice makers utilize ambient air to cool the refrigeration system, making them more energy-efficient and suitable for environments with adequate ventilation. In contrast, water-cooled ice makers rely on water to dissipate heat, making them ideal for locations with limited airflow and higher cooling demands. These types cater to various commercial needs, from restaurants to hotels, depending on space, efficiency, and cooling requirements.</p></p>
